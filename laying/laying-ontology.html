Laying authority typec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/laying/LayingAuthorityType

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/laying/
The authority under which a paper is laid before a House. Legislation may impose a duty on a body to lay Act papers before a House, there are referred to as being laid 'by Act'. Command papers are laid in the House by the government and are referred to as being laid 'by Command'. A paper is laid either 'by Act' or 'by Command', and may be laid 'by Act' in the Commons and 'by Command' in the Lords.

Sessionc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/laying/Session

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/laying/
The period during which Parliament is convened. The first session of a parliament begins on the same day as the parliament period: this date is given in the dissolution proclamation at the end of the previous parliament. The session ends on the day given in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the parliament begin and end on dates given in prorogation proclamations. The final session of a parliament ends with a prorogation followed by a dissolution. A session typically lasts for one year though isn't bound by that. Session periods are the same across both Houses.
is in range of
In sessionop
